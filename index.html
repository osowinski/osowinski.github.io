01 — About

Background &
Who I am

My journey into IT didn't start in a classroom — it started with a controller in my hand and a curiosity about what was happening inside the machine. Growing up gaming with my dad sparked something that turned into a genuine obsession with how technology works at a fundamental level.

At 15, I built my first PC from scratch. No shortcuts — just components, patience, and a lot of learning from mistakes. That experience taught me more than any textbook could: how hardware thinks, how systems fail, and how satisfying it is to make something work properly.

My high school didn't offer Computer Science, so I self-taught. Personal projects, late nights reading documentation, hands-on builds. By the time I reached university, I already had a foundation most students were only starting to build.

Outside of tech, I'm someone who stays curious and active. Formula 1 is a genuine passion — long-term, I'd love to find a way to bring my IT career into the world of motorsport. Beyond that, you'll find me watching football or tennis, training in martial arts, or with a camera in hand somewhere worth photographing. I'm happiest in the mountains — hiking in the summer, skiing in winter — and I have a soft spot for dogs, particularly Dachshunds.

02 — Goals

Goals &
Aspirations

I'm at a point in my career where I know the direction I want to head, even if the path isn't fully mapped out yet.

Formula 1 has been a passion of mine for as long as I can remember, and the more I learn about the sport the more I realise how deeply technology runs through it. The engineering, the software, the sheer volume of data processed in real time — it's a world where IT and programming are absolutely central. Whether that leads me toward tooling, systems, development or something else entirely, finding a way to connect my career to motorsport is something I genuinely aspire to.

Basketball is another sport that's opened my eyes to how much programming can reveal. The NBA is full of stories hidden inside statistics — and being able to write code that surfaces those stories is something I find genuinely exciting. It's less about a specific job title and more about the craft of using programming to explore things I care about.

More broadly, I want to keep growing as a programmer. I'm curious about a lot — different languages, different problem spaces, different ways of building things. I'd rather stay open and keep exploring than narrow my focus too soon.

I also have ambitions to go back into education further down the line — a different degree, and potentially a PhD one day. There's a lot of world left to learn about, and I'm in no rush.
